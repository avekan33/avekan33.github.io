Greetings!

My name's Averal, and I am in the business of learning and making things. I am currently a student at UC Berkeley, studying Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences with an intended focus in the design of integrated circuits for signal processing. To this end, I have taken / am taking courses in computer architecture, signal processing, probability and random processes, control, MEMS, device physics, and digital and analog circuits.

I enjoy challenges and look to solve tough problems in my classwork, research, extracurricular activities, and projects (see below!).

I've wanted to be an engineer since before I understood what engineering was, and my progress thus far in the field has only amplified this desire. My ultimate aim is to produce work that will benefit humanity as a whole: in particular, since June 2019, I have been working on a project in Michel Maharbiz's research group with postdoc Soner Sonmezoglu which aims to enable reliable digital communication with implanted sensors via pieoelectric ultrasonic backscatter modulation. This work has the potential to revolutionize the design of bio-sensors and implanted chips, and I'm hoping to build on this potential by designing wearable, data-efficient ultrasonic transceiver systems in the future.
